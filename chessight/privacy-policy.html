Privacy Policy for Chessight

Last updated: April 24, 2026
Chessight uses limited data for app functionality, premium purchase entitlements, analytics, diagnostics, and advertising as described below.

Data Collection

  • The app does not require user accounts or login.
  • Chess.com usernames entered by the user are used solely to fetch publicly available game data from the Chess.com public API.
  • We do not receive your payment card or billing details. Apple processes purchases through StoreKit.
  • We and our service providers may process limited data needed to provide the app, measure usage, diagnose crashes, support rewarded ads, and verify premium entitlements.

Data Categories We Collect

  • Purchases: Product identifiers and transaction dates used to determine whether premium access is active.
  • Identifiers: Advertising identifier (IDFA) if you grant tracking permission, and device identifiers used by our service providers.
  • Usage Data: Screen views, app events, and feature usage.
  • Diagnostics: Crash logs and performance data.
  • Device Information: Device model, iOS version, and app version.

Payments

We offer an optional one-time premium purchase processed by Apple. We never receive your payment method. Apple handles transactions through StoreKit. We record only the information needed to determine whether your purchase entitlement is active, such as the product identifier and transaction date. Apple's privacy policy applies to the payment itself.

Advertising

We display rewarded video ads through Google AdMob for users who have not purchased premium. Google may collect data as an independent controller for ad delivery and measurement, including the advertising identifier (IDFA) if you grant permission through App Tracking Transparency, IP address, coarse location, device type and operating system, and ad interaction data such as when an ad is shown, clicked, or completed.

If you decline tracking permission, ads may still appear but they will be non-personalized. Learn more at Google's advertising privacy policy.

Tracking Permission

When you first launch the app, iOS may ask whether to allow tracking. We use this permission only to enable personalized ads. You can change this choice at any time in iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Tracking. Denying tracking does not disable any app functionality.

Third-Party Services

  • Google AdMob: Provides rewarded advertising and ad measurement. See Google's advertising privacy policy.
  • Apple StoreKit: Processes premium purchases and supports purchase verification. See Apple's privacy policy.
  • Firebase Crashlytics: Collects crash reports and performance data to help us improve app stability. See Firebase privacy information.
  • Firebase Analytics: Collects usage statistics such as screen views, app events, and feature usage. See Firebase privacy information.
  • Chess.com Public API: Used to fetch publicly available player profiles and game data. See Chess.com privacy policy.

Data Storage

  • All data (saved profiles, cached analyses, engine settings) is stored locally on your device.
  • Analytics, diagnostics, advertising, and purchase entitlement data may be processed by the third-party services listed above.

Your Choices

  • You can choose whether to allow tracking in iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Tracking.
  • You can manage Apple's ad preferences in iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Apple Advertising and turn Personalized Ads on or off.
  • You can avoid rewarded ads by purchasing premium.

Children

Chessight is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal data through the app, please contact us so we can review and address the issue.

Changes In This Update

  • Added details about App Tracking Transparency permission and how to change that setting in iOS.
  • Updated the list of third-party services and the categories of data processed by the app and its service providers.
